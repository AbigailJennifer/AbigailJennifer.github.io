Computational Chemist | f-Block Electronic Structure

Bio

Abigail is a computational chemist working at the intersection of electronic structure theory and complex chemical systems involving f-block elements. Her research focuses on developing and applying advanced quantum chemical methods to understand bonding, reactivity, and spectroscopy in actinide and lanthanide complexes.

Her work integrates density functional theory and multiconfigurational approaches to address challenges in strongly correlated systems, with particular emphasis on interpreting X-ray absorption spectra and periodic trends across the actinide series. Her research outcomes aim in bridging theoretical insight with experimentally relevant observables.

Research Interests

  • Electronic structure of heavy-element based complexes
  • Multiconfigurational methods
  • X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS) and core-level excitations
  • Relativistic effects in heavy-element chemistry
  • Periodicty in electronic structure properties of actinide complexes
  • Ligand design for selective actinide separation
  • Chemical Education
