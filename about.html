Abigail Jennifer

Computational Chemist | f-Block Electronic Structure

Abigail is a computational chemist working on electronic structure theory of complex chemical systems involving f-block elements. Her research focuses on developing and applying advanced quantum chemical methods to understand bonding, reactivity, and spectroscopy in actinide and lanthanide complexes.

Her work integrates density functional theory and multiconfigurational approaches to address challenges in strongly correlated systems, with particular emphasis on interpreting X-ray absorption spectra and periodic trends across the actinide series.

Research Interests
  • Electronic structure of heavy-element complexes
  • Multiconfigurational methods
  • X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS)
  • Relativistic quantum chemistry
  • Actinide periodicity
  • Ligand design for selectivity
  • Chemical education
